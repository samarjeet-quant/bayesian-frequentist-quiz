Bayesian vs Frequentist

Answer honestly. There are no right or wrong answers.
Read the questions carefully.

1. You believed something strongly. You get new evidence contradicting your belief. Do you update your belief?

2. You flip a coin 3 times and get HHH. Do you suspect the coin might be biased?

3. A disease is so rare that it affects only 1 in 1000 people. The test for this disease is 99% accurate. You test positive. How much do you believe are the chances that you have the disease?

4. You would meet atleast 20 people you know at this fest. Can you assign a probability to that event?

5. The canteen 'samosa' has been cold most of the times in the past. Today, someone says it's fresh. Do you still assume a high chance that it's cold?

6. You hear a rumour from one person. Does it affect your belief at all?

7. You hear one dramatic failure story about a product. But thousands of people use it successfully. Does the story affect your belief?

8. A player performs poorly in one match. Do you think their form is declining?

9. You skip exercise for a week. Do you worry you are losing the habit?

10. You know you've had bad experiences in the past. Do you still let that influence your current decisions?

11. Before the results of an examination, you feel you have done well. Does that affect what you believe the outcome will be?

12. You've had unusually GOOD luck in the past week. Do you expect it to continue?

13. The famous "Monty Hall" problem. You are given three doors to choose from. Behind two of them is a goat, but behind one is a car. You choose a door. The host opens another door, revealing a goat. Do you switch doors or remain with the one you've chosen?

14. There is no data for tomorrow's weather, just experience and hunches. Can you still say, "there's a 30% chance of rain"?

15. You know most people enjoy a particular movie. One person you trust hates it. Does that matter a lot to you?
